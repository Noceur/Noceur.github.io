More Terrains & Procedural Workflows 05.03.2021

Continuing my exploration into procedural generation, I've been diving deeper into asset assembly and mesh generation with Houdini. The non-destructive nature that allows for easy revisions and ability to easily reuse and apply subnetworks/HDAs has the potiential to be an amazing time saver (at cost of extra overhead setup) while also ensuring a more even base level quality.

+

Terrains & World creator

However while it provides great tools for scattering assets and as a procedural powerhouse my current opinion is that it lacks in terms of terrains. The workflow and steps required to properly handle the heightfields themselves felt a bit unintuitive and I feel it hampers creativity.

Terrains generated in Houdini



As I noted in a earlier post the easiest path I've tried have been to use World Creator to create the terrain and process the landscape further with Houdini. The downside is however quite clear as it adds another package to the pipeline as WC has no chance of replacing Houdini entirely. Without PC AAA experience I can't say for sure but I would assume most open world games use a similar setup.

Terrains made in world creator 2022



Here I've taken the terrain created above in World Creator and scattered some very simple meshes representing trees and generated a mesh for a river. One of my current projects is looking into adjusting the terrain heightmap for rivers in Houdini instead of just taking the river defined in WC, the reason being that it is easier to create cascading effects and procedurally placing particle effects among other things. There was a breakdown by Guerilla Games on their own procedural river workflow which I would like to try and replicate in the future.

Powerlines & Cables

The following is some experimentation at creating a cable and a powerline generator, the idea being that you can draw a curve on a terrain, drag a few sliders and have it output both the powerlines and remove the bigger foliage that wouldn't be allowed to exist in proximity.



Here the trees have been extracted by the same curve used to generate the powerlines.



The next step would be to add a wire solver to create the wires. I already have a solver solution but need a way of identifying what points on the lines are attached to the poles, something to explore in the future.
